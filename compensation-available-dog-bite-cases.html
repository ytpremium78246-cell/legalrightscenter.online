What Compensation Is Available in Dog Bite Cases?

Explore the damages available for dog attacks, reconstructive surgery, and trauma.

Reconstructive Surgery and Scarring Damages

Dog bites often cause lacerations, torn muscles, and nerve damage, particularly on the face, hands, and arms. Recoverable damages include the cost of immediate emergency care, rabies vaccines, and future plastic surgery to minimize permanent scarring.

Psychological Trauma and PTSD Support

Dog attacks, especially those involving children, cause severe emotional trauma. Compensation is available for mental health counseling, physical therapy, fear of dogs, sleep disorders, and PTSD caused by the attack.

Economic Losses and Wage Recovery

If your injuries prevent you from working, you can recover lost wages. If the attack causes permanent nerve damage or loss of use of a hand, you can claim loss of earning capacity and physical impairment damages.

Case Study Analysis: Dog Bite Liability and Homeowner's Insurance Exclusions

A child suffered severe facial lacerations and nerve damage after being attacked by a neighbor's dog on a public sidewalk. The neighbor's homeowner's insurance carrier denied coverage, citing a specific 'vicious breed' exclusion in the policy. The child's parents, represented by counsel, pursued a personal injury claim against the owner, asserting strict liability under the state's dog bite statute.

The attorney conducted an asset search and confirmed the owner had sufficient personal assets, including equity in a secondary property. Facing a direct lawsuit that threatened their personal holdings, the owner retained independent counsel and agreed to a structured settlement paid through a combination of personal funds and a negotiated contribution from their insurer. This case emphasizes that while breed exclusions present insurance challenges, strict liability statutes still hold the owner personally responsible for all damages.

Step-by-Step Action Plan: Handling a Dog Bite Incident

Step 1: Identify the Owner and the Dog

Obtain the owner's name, address, phone number, and homeowner's or renter's insurance details. Ask for proof of the dog's rabies vaccination status to protect your health.

Step 2: Seek Immediate Emergency Medical Care

Wash the wound with soap and water immediately, and visit an urgent care or ER. Animal bites carry a high risk of infection; get professional cleaning, stitches, and vaccines.

Step 3: Report the Attack to Animal Control

File an official report with county animal control or the health department. They will quarantine the animal, verify its vaccine status, and compile an official report documenting the attack history.

Step 4: Photograph the Wounds and Scene

Take high-resolution photos of your injuries immediately, during treatment, and throughout the healing process to document permanent scarring, nerve damage, or bruising.

Step 5: Submit a Claim to the Insurer

Have your lawyer file a liability claim with the owner's homeowner's or renter's insurance company to recover compensation for medical bills, cosmetic surgery, and emotional trauma.

Legal Glossary: Key Terms Explained

Strict Liability
A legal standard holding a dog owner responsible for injuries caused by a dog bite, regardless of the dog's past behavior or owner's care.
One-Bite Rule
The common law doctrine holding that a dog owner is only liable if they had prior notice that the dog had dangerous or vicious propensities.
Homeowner's Breed Exclusion
Insurance policy terms excluding liability coverage for dog bites from specific breeds (e.g., Pit Bulls or Rottweilers).

Finding Local Personal Injury Assistance

If you are seeking financial recovery after a severe injury, consulting a local personal injury attorney can clarify your rights.
