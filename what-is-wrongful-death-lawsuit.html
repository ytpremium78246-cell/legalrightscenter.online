What Is a Wrongful Death Lawsuit?

Explore the legal definition, elements of proof, and procedures in wrongful death cases.

Legal Definition of Wrongful Death

A wrongful death lawsuit is a civil action filed when a person dies due to the negligence, recklessness, or intentional misconduct of another party. It allows survivors to seek financial compensation for their loss.

Elements of Proof in Fatal Claims

To succeed, you must prove the identical elements of negligence as if the victim survived: (1) the defendant owed a duty of care; (2) they breached that duty; (3) the breach caused the death; and (4) the survivors suffered measurable damages.

Sovereign Immunity and Government Wrongful Death

If the death was caused by a government employee or dangerous road design, sovereign immunity rules apply. You must file an administrative claim within a very short deadline (90-180 days) before suing the government.

Key Legal Codes & Statutory Analysis

The Statutory Framework of Wrongful Death and Survival Actions

When an accident is fatal, the deceased's family members cannot file a standard personal injury claim. Instead, they must file a Wrongful Death action, which is a statutory claim designed to compensate specific close relatives (spouse, children, parents) for their personal losses, including lost financial support, funeral expenses, and loss of companionship. In tandem, a Survival Action is filed on behalf of the deceased's estate to recover damages for the physical pain, suffering, and medical bills incurred by the deceased between the time of the injury and the moment of death, requiring careful legal separation of assets.

Statutory Authority: State domestic relations and probate codes.

In personal injury and civil litigation, authorities like State domestic relations and probate codes. define the standards of care, liability rules, and deadlines that govern claims. These laws establish who is legally responsible for accidents and how financial damages (medical bills, lost income, pain and suffering) are calculated. Injured plaintiffs must compile evidence showing a violation of these standards to recover compensation from liability insurers or negligent corporations.

Case Study Analysis: Wrongful Death and Survival Action Allocations

Following a fatal industrial equipment accident, the surviving spouse and children filed a wrongful death and survival lawsuit against the machinery manufacturer, alleging a design defect. The manufacturer argued that the operator was negligent, but engineering experts showed that the machine lacked a standard safety interlock switch that would have prevented the fatal sequence.

The case settled before trial, requiring the court to approve the allocation of the settlement proceeds. The judge divided the recovery between the Wrongful Death Claim (compensating the spouse and children for loss of support, companionship, and funeral costs) and the Survival Action (compensating the estate for the deceased's pre-death pain and suffering and medical bills). This allocation was critical for tax and estate distribution purposes, illustrating the distinct legal pathways required in fatal accident litigation.

Step-by-Step Action Plan: Initiating a Wrongful Death Claim

Step 1: Open the Deceased's Estate

Petition the probate court to appoint an executor or personal representative for the deceased's estate. This representative has the legal standing to file a survival action on behalf of the estate.

Step 2: Identify Eligible Beneficiaries

Determine which surviving family members (such as spouses, children, or parents) are entitled to receive damages under your state's wrongful death statute.

Step 3: Compile Complete Records

Gather emergency medical charts, coroner's reports, autopsy records, death certificates, and police reports documenting the fatal accident and its cause.

Step 4: Calculate Economic and Future Loss

Hire financial experts or economists to calculate the lost lifetime earnings and benefits of the deceased, along with funeral and burial expenses.

Step 5: File the Civil Action Promptly

Have your attorney file a wrongful death and survival action in civil court before the statute of limitations expires, which is often shorter than general personal injury claims.

Legal Glossary: Key Terms Explained

Wrongful Death Claim
A statutory lawsuit filed by surviving relatives to recover compensation for their personal losses resulting from a family member's death.
Survival Action
A claim filed by the executor of the deceased's estate to recover damages for the pain, suffering, and medical bills the deceased experienced before death.
Intestate Succession
The state statutes dictating how estate property is distributed when a person dies without leaving a valid will.

Finding Local Personal Injury Assistance

If you are seeking financial recovery after a severe injury, consulting a local personal injury attorney can clarify your rights. Most civil injury lawyers operate on a contingency fee basis, meaning they evaluate and handle cases with no upfront costs, taking a percentage of the final recovery. You can verify attorney credentials and disciplinary histories through your state's bar association portal. For immediate support, consult local legal clinics or check hospital social work departments, which help injured patients apply for temporary disability benefits, MedPay coverage, or regional medical care programs.
